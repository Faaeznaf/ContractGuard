                    EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT


This Employment Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the 
15th day of July, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

     FinTech Solutions Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal 
     place of business at 2500 Financial Plaza, Austin, TX 78701 
     (hereinafter referred to as "Company" or "Employer"),

and

     Jane Doe, an individual residing at 123 Oak Street, Austin, TX 78702 
     (hereinafter referred to as "Employee").


                              RECITALS

WHEREAS, Company desires to employ Employee as a Software Engineer; and

WHEREAS, Employee desires to accept such employment upon the terms and 
conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements 
contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the 
receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the parties 
agree as follows:


1. EMPLOYMENT

   1.1 Position. Company hereby employs Employee, and Employee hereby 
       accepts employment with Company, in the position of Software 
       Engineer, Level 3. Employee shall report to the Chief Technology 
       Officer or such other person as the Company may designate.

   1.2 Duties and Responsibilities. Employee shall perform such duties and 
       responsibilities as are customarily associated with the position of 
       Software Engineer and as may be assigned from time to time by the 
       Company. Employee's duties shall include, but not be limited to:

       (a) Designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software 
           applications and systems for the Company's financial technology 
           platform;

       (b) Writing clean, efficient, and well-documented code;

       (c) Collaborating with cross-functional teams including product 
           managers, designers, and other engineers;

       (d) Participating in code reviews, technical design discussions, 
           and agile development processes;

       (e) Troubleshooting and resolving software defects and production 
           issues;

       (f) Staying current with emerging technologies and industry best 
           practices;

       (g) Other duties as reasonably assigned by the Company.

   1.3 At-Will Employment. Employee's employment with Company is "at-will," 
       meaning that either Employee or Company may terminate the 
       employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and 
       with or without notice, subject to the notice provisions in 
       Section 7 of this Agreement. Nothing in this Agreement shall be 
       construed to create a contract for a specific term of employment.

   1.4 Work Location. Employee's primary work location shall be at the 
       Company's offices in Austin, Texas. However, Company acknowledges 
       and agrees that Employee is permitted to work remotely on a full-
       time basis, subject to Company's remote work policies and 
       procedures. Employee may be required to attend in-person meetings, 
       team events, or training sessions as reasonably requested by 
       Company with adequate advance notice.

   1.5 Full-Time Commitment. Employee shall devote Employee's full 
       business time, attention, skills, and efforts to the performance of 
       Employee's duties hereunder. Employee shall not, without the prior 
       written consent of Company, engage in any other business activity 
       that conflicts or interferes with Employee's duties and obligations 
       to Company.


2. COMPENSATION

   2.1 Base Salary. Employee shall receive an annual base salary of One 
       Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($150,000.00), payable in accordance 
       with Company's standard payroll practices, currently bi-weekly. 
       Employee's salary shall be subject to applicable withholdings and 
       deductions required by law or authorized by Employee.

   2.2 Performance Reviews and Adjustments. Employee's performance and 
       compensation shall be reviewed annually. Any adjustments to 
       Employee's compensation shall be at the sole discretion of Company 
       based on factors including but not limited to Employee's 
       performance, Company performance, and market conditions.

   2.3 Bonus Eligibility. Employee shall be eligible to participate in 
       Company's annual bonus program, with a target bonus of fifteen 
       percent (15%) of base salary, subject to achievement of individual 
       and company performance objectives as determined by Company in its 
       sole discretion. Bonus payments, if any, shall be made in 
       accordance with the terms of the applicable bonus plan.

   2.4 Equity Compensation. Subject to approval by the Company's Board of 
       Directors or Compensation Committee, Employee shall be granted 
       stock options to purchase 10,000 shares of Company common stock, 
       pursuant to the Company's 2024 Equity Incentive Plan. The terms of 
       such stock options, including vesting schedule, exercise price, and 
       other conditions, shall be set forth in a separate stock option 
       agreement.


3. BENEFITS

   3.1 Employee Benefits. Employee shall be eligible to participate in all 
       employee benefit plans, programs, and arrangements maintained by 
       Company for its employees, including:

       (a) Health Insurance: Company shall provide comprehensive health 
           insurance coverage, including medical, dental, and vision 
           benefits. Company shall pay 100% of the premium for employee-
           only coverage and 75% of the premium for dependent coverage;

       (b) Retirement Plan: Employee shall be eligible to participate in 
           Company's 401(k) retirement plan with company matching 
           contributions of up to 5% of base salary;

       (c) Life and Disability Insurance: Company shall provide basic life 
           insurance coverage equal to one times annual base salary and 
           long-term disability insurance;

       (d) Paid Time Off: Employee shall be entitled to twenty (20) days 
           of paid time off (PTO) per calendar year, accruing in 
           accordance with Company policy. Unused PTO may be carried over 
           to the following year subject to a maximum accrual cap as 
           specified in Company policy;

       (e) Holidays: Employee shall be entitled to all Company-observed 
           holidays, currently ten (10) paid holidays per year;

       (f) Sick Leave: Employee shall be entitled to sick leave in 
           accordance with applicable state and local law;

       (g) Parental Leave: Employee shall be eligible for parental leave 
           benefits in accordance with Company policy, currently providing 
           twelve (12) weeks of paid parental leave;

       (h) Professional Development: Company shall provide up to $3,000 
           annually for professional development expenses, including 
           conferences, training courses, and certifications, subject to 
           prior approval.

   3.2 Benefit Plan Terms. All benefits are subject to the terms and 
       conditions of the applicable benefit plans and Company policies, 
       which may be modified by Company from time to time in its sole 
       discretion.


4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ASSIGNMENT

   4.1 Work Product. Employee acknowledges and agrees that all inventions, 
       discoveries, developments, improvements, works of authorship, 
       software, code, designs, formulas, processes, techniques, know-how, 
       data, trade secrets, and other intellectual property of any kind 
       (collectively, "Work Product") that Employee conceives, develops, 
       creates, or reduces to practice, either alone or jointly with 
       others, during the term of employment shall be the sole and 
       exclusive property of Company.

   4.2 Scope of Assignment. Without limiting the generality of Section 
       4.1, Employee hereby assigns to Company all right, title, and 
       interest in and to all Work Product, including all patent rights, 
       copyrights, trademark rights, trade secret rights, and other 
       intellectual property rights therein. This assignment applies to 
       all Work Product:

       (a) That relates to Company's actual or anticipated business, 
           research, or development;

       (b) That results from any work performed by Employee for Company;

       (c) That is developed using Company's equipment, supplies, 
           facilities, time, or confidential information;

       (d) That is developed during working hours or otherwise during the 
           course of Employee's employment; or

       (e) That relates to or is suggested by any work that Employee 
           performs for Company.

   4.3 Prior Inventions. Employee has disclosed to Company on Exhibit A 
       attached hereto a complete list of all inventions, discoveries, and 
       improvements that Employee made prior to employment with Company 
       and that Employee desires to exclude from the scope of this 
       Agreement (collectively, "Prior Inventions"). If no such list is 
       attached, Employee represents that there are no such Prior 
       Inventions. Any Prior Inventions shall remain the sole property of 
       Employee.

   4.4 Further Assurances. Employee agrees to execute and deliver such 
       applications, assignments, and other documents, and to take such 
       other actions, as Company may reasonably request to evidence, 
       perfect, or defend Company's ownership of Work Product, including 
       execution of patent applications, copyright registrations, and 
       assignments.

   4.5 Moral Rights. To the extent permitted by law, Employee hereby 
       waives any moral rights, rights of attribution, or similar rights 
       in any Work Product.


5. NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

   5.1 Non-Compete. During Employee's employment with Company and for a 
       period of two (2) years following the termination of Employee's 
       employment for any reason (the "Restricted Period"), Employee shall 
       not, directly or indirectly, whether as an employee, consultant, 
       independent contractor, owner, partner, officer, director, or 
       otherwise:

       (a) Engage in, provide services to, or have any financial interest 
           in any business that competes with Company's business of 
           providing financial technology software, payment processing 
           systems, digital banking solutions, financial data analytics, 
           or related financial services products (a "Competing Business");

       (b) Work for or provide services to any company or entity that 
           offers products or services that compete with any product or 
           service offered by Company or under development by Company 
           during Employee's employment.

   5.2 Geographic Scope. The non-compete restrictions in Section 5.1 shall 
       apply to: (a) the United States of America; (b) any country in 
       which Company conducts business or has customers at the time of 
       Employee's termination; and (c) any country in which Company has 
       taken demonstrable steps to conduct business.

   5.3 Non-Solicitation of Employees. During the Restricted Period, 
       Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, 
       or encourage any employee or independent contractor of Company to 
       leave their employment or engagement with Company or to accept 
       employment or engagement with any other person or entity.

   5.4 Non-Solicitation of Customers. During the Restricted Period, 
       Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or 
       accept business from any customer, client, or prospective customer 
       or client of Company with whom Employee had contact or about whom 
       Employee obtained confidential information during Employee's 
       employment for the purpose of providing products or services that 
       compete with Company's products or services.

   5.5 Reasonableness. Employee acknowledges and agrees that the 
       restrictions contained in this Section 5 are reasonable and 
       necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests, 
       including its confidential information, customer relationships, and 
       goodwill, and that such restrictions do not impose an undue burden 
       on Employee.


6. CONFIDENTIALITY

   6.1 Confidential Information. Employee acknowledges that during the 
       course of employment, Employee will have access to and learn about 
       confidential, secret, and proprietary information of Company, 
       including but not limited to:

       (a) Technical information, such as software, source code, 
           algorithms, architectures, data structures, system designs, 
           development tools, and technical specifications;

       (b) Business information, such as business plans, strategies, 
           financial information, pricing, costs, customer lists, supplier 
           lists, marketing plans, and sales data;

       (c) Product information, such as product roadmaps, features under 
           development, and product specifications;

       (d) Personnel information, such as employee compensation, 
           performance reviews, and hiring plans;

       (e) Any other information that Company treats as confidential or 
           that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential 
           given the nature of the information and circumstances of 
           disclosure.

       All such information is collectively referred to as "Confidential 
       Information."

   6.2 Non-Disclosure. Employee agrees not to disclose any Confidential 
       Information to any person or entity, except as required to perform 
       Employee's duties for Company or as authorized in writing by 
       Company. Employee agrees to use Confidential Information only for 
       the benefit of Company and not for Employee's own benefit or the 
       benefit of any third party.

   6.3 Protection of Confidential Information. Employee agrees to take all 
       reasonable steps to protect Confidential Information and to prevent 
       its unauthorized disclosure or use. Employee shall immediately 
       notify Company of any unauthorized disclosure or use of 
       Confidential Information of which Employee becomes aware.

   6.4 Survival. Employee's obligations under this Section 6 shall survive 
       the termination of Employee's employment and shall continue 
       indefinitely, or until such time as the Confidential Information 
       becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement by 
       Employee.

   6.5 Exceptions. The obligations set forth in this Section 6 shall not 
       apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available 
       through no breach of this Agreement by Employee; (b) was rightfully 
       in Employee's possession prior to disclosure by Company; (c) is 
       rightfully received by Employee from a third party without breach 
       of any confidentiality obligation; or (d) is independently 
       developed by Employee without use of Confidential Information.

   6.6 Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice. Pursuant to the Defend Trade 
       Secrets Act of 2016, Employee is notified that: (a) an individual 
       shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or 
       state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is 
       made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government 
       official or to an attorney solely for the purpose of reporting or 
       investigating a suspected violation of law; (b) an individual who 
       files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a 
       suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the 
       attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in 
       the court proceeding, if the individual files any document 
       containing the trade secret under seal and does not disclose the 
       trade secret except pursuant to court order.


7. TERMINATION

   7.1 Termination Notice. Either party may terminate Employee's 
       employment at any time for any reason or no reason. However, if 
       either party chooses to terminate the employment relationship 
       (other than for Cause as defined below), such party shall provide 
       the other party with at least two (2) weeks' advance written 
       notice.

   7.2 Termination for Cause. Notwithstanding Section 7.1, Company may 
       terminate Employee's employment immediately for Cause without 
       notice or payment in lieu of notice. "Cause" means:

       (a) Employee's material breach of this Agreement;

       (b) Employee's willful misconduct, gross negligence, or dishonesty;

       (c) Employee's conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, 
           any felony or crime involving moral turpitude;

       (d) Employee's material violation of Company policies or procedures;

       (e) Employee's unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential 
           Information;

       (f) Employee's failure to perform assigned duties after written 
           warning and reasonable opportunity to cure; or

       (g) Any other conduct that is materially harmful to Company's 
           business, reputation, or interests.

   7.3 Compensation Upon Termination. Upon termination of employment for 
       any reason:

       (a) Company shall pay Employee all earned but unpaid salary through 
           the date of termination;

       (b) Company shall pay Employee for all accrued but unused PTO in 
           accordance with Company policy and applicable law;

       (c) All other benefits shall cease as of the termination date, 
           except as required by law or the terms of the applicable 
           benefit plans;

       (d) Any unvested equity compensation shall be forfeited;

       (e) Employee shall have the right to exercise vested stock options 
           in accordance with the terms of the applicable stock option 
           agreement.

   7.4 Return of Company Property. Upon termination, Employee shall 
       immediately return to Company all Company property, including 
       without limitation: computers, mobile devices, keys, access cards, 
       documents, files, equipment, and any other property belonging to 
       Company. Employee shall also permanently delete or destroy all 
       Confidential Information in Employee's possession and certify such 
       deletion or destruction in writing to Company.

   7.5 Survival. The provisions of Sections 4 (Intellectual Property 
       Assignment), 5 (Non-Compete and Non-Solicitation), 6 
       (Confidentiality), 8 (General Provisions), and any other provisions 
       that by their nature should survive termination shall survive the 
       termination of this Agreement and Employee's employment.


8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

   8.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including any exhibits attached 
       hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties 
       regarding Employee's employment and supersedes all prior 
       negotiations, agreements, understandings, and communications, 
       whether written or oral.

   8.2 Amendments. No amendment or modification of this Agreement shall be 
       effective unless in writing and signed by both parties.

   8.3 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in 
       accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to 
       its conflict of laws principles.

   8.4 Jurisdiction and Venue. The parties hereby consent to the exclusive 
       jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in 
       Travis County, Texas for any disputes arising under or relating to 
       this Agreement.

   8.5 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be 
       invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall 
       remain in full force and effect. If any restriction in Section 5 is 
       held to be unreasonable or unenforceable, the court is requested 
       and authorized to modify the restriction to make it reasonable and 
       enforceable.

   8.6 Waiver. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be 
       effective unless in writing and signed by the party against whom 
       such waiver is sought to be enforced.

   8.7 Assignment. Employee may not assign this Agreement. Company may 
       assign this Agreement to any successor or affiliate without 
       Employee's consent.

   8.8 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and 
       shall be deemed delivered when delivered personally, sent by 
       confirmed electronic mail, or three (3) business days after being 
       sent by certified mail, return receipt requested.

   8.9 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each 
       of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together 
       shall constitute one and the same instrument.
