VENDOR SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Vendor Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of
the 15th day of March, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

  TechCorp Inc. a Delaware corporation with its principal place of
  business at 123 Technology Drive, San Francisco, CA 94105 (hereinafter
  referred to as "Client" or "Buyer"),

and

  CloudSupplier LLC, a Texas limited liability company with its
  principal place of business at 456 Cloud Avenue, Austin, TX 78701
  (hereinafter referred to as "Vendor" or "Supplier").

Client and Vendor are collectively referred to herein as the "Parties"
and individually as a "Party."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Vendor provides cloud infrastructure and managed services; and

WHEREAS, Client desires to engage Vendor to provide certain services as
described herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises
contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the
receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties
agree as follows:

1.  SERVICES

1.  Scope of Services. Vendor shall provide Client with cloud
    infrastructure services, including but not limited to virtual server
    provisioning, data storage solutions, network configuration,
    security monitoring, backup and disaster recovery services, and
    technical support (collectively, the "Services").

2.  Service Standards. Vendor shall perform all Services in a
    professional and workmanlike manner in accordance with industry
    standards. Vendor shall provide Services with an uptime guarantee of
    ninety-nine point nine percent (99.9%) measured on a monthly basis.

3.  Service Modifications. Client may request modifications to the
    Services by providing written notice to Vendor. Any material changes
    to the scope of Services shall require a written amendment executed
    by both Parties.

2.  PAYMENT TERMS

4.  Fees. Client shall pay Vendor monthly fees based on actual resource
    consumption and service utilization as set forth in Exhibit A
    attached hereto. Vendor reserves the right to adjust fees annually
    upon thirty (30) days' written notice to Client.

5.  Payment Terms. All invoices shall be paid within sixty (60) days of
    the invoice date ("Net 60"). Late payments shall accrue interest at
    a rate of one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month or the maximum
    rate permitted by law, whichever is less.

6.  Taxes. Client shall be responsible for all applicable federal,
    state, and local taxes, duties, and assessments arising from this
    Agreement, excluding taxes based on Vendor's net income.

3.  TERM AND TERMINATION

7.  Initial Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date
    and shall continue for an initial term of three (3) years, unless
    earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions herein.

8.  Renewal. Upon expiration of the initial term, this Agreement shall
    automatically renew for successive one (1) year terms unless either
    Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred
    eighty (180) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

9.  Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for
    cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any
    provision hereof and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30)
    days after receiving written notice thereof.

10. Effect of Termination. Upon termination, Client shall pay all
    outstanding fees for Services rendered through the termination date.
    Vendor shall cooperate with Client to ensure orderly transition of
    Services.

4.  INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

11. Pre-Existing Intellectual Property. Each Party retains all right,
    title, and interest in and to its pre-existing intellectual
    property, including but not limited to patents, copyrights,
    trademarks, trade secrets, and proprietary technologies.

12. Joint Intellectual Property. Any intellectual property, including
    inventions, works of authorship, software, processes, or
    methodologies that are jointly developed or created by both Parties
    in connection with the performance of Services under this Agreement
    shall be jointly owned by both Parties. Each Party shall have the
    right to use, modify, license, and commercialize such jointly owned
    intellectual property without restriction and without accounting to
    the other Party, subject to the confidentiality obligations set
    forth herein.

13. License Grant. Vendor grants to Client a non-exclusive,
    non-transferable license to use Vendor's proprietary software and
    tools solely for the purpose of receiving the Services during the
    term of this Agreement.

5.  LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

14. Unlimited Liability. VENDOR SHALL BE LIABLE TO CLIENT FOR ALL
    DAMAGES, LOSSES, COSTS, AND EXPENSES OF ANY KIND ARISING OUT OF OR
    RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT OR THE SERVICES PROVIDED HEREUNDER,
    INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO DIRECT DAMAGES, INDIRECT DAMAGES,
    CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCIDENTAL DAMAGES, SPECIAL DAMAGES, LOST
    PROFITS, LOSS OF DATA, BUSINESS INTERRUPTION, AND ANY OTHER ECONOMIC
    LOSSES, WHETHER BASED IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE),
    STRICT LIABILITY, OR OTHERWISE. THERE SHALL BE NO LIMITATION OR CAP
    ON VENDOR'S LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT.

15. Indemnification by Vendor. Vendor shall indemnify, defend, and hold
    harmless Client, its affiliates, and their respective officers,
    directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all
    claims, demands, losses, costs, damages, liabilities, judgments, and
    expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or
    relating to: (a) any breach of this Agreement by Vendor; (b) any
    negligent or wrongful act or omission by Vendor; (c) any claim that
    Vendor's Services or technology infringe any intellectual property
    right; or (d) any violation of applicable law by Vendor.

16. Insurance. Vendor shall maintain, at its own expense, comprehensive
    general liability insurance, professional liability insurance, and
    cyber liability insurance with coverage limits of not less than five
    million dollars ($5,000,000) per occurrence and in the aggregate.

6.  CONFIDENTIALITY

17. Confidential Information. Each Party acknowledges that it may
    receive or have access to confidential and proprietary information
    of the other Party, including but not limited to technical data,
    trade secrets, business plans, customer lists, financial
    information, and other non-public information ("Confidential
    Information").

18. Confidentiality Obligations. Each Party agrees to: (a) maintain the
    confidentiality of the other Party's Confidential Information; (b)
    not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party
    without prior written consent; and (c) use such Confidential
    Information solely for the purposes of this Agreement. These
    obligations shall survive for a period of two (2) years following
    the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

19. Exceptions. Confidential Information shall not include information
    that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this
    Agreement; (b) is rightfully received from a third party without
    breach of confidentiality obligations; (c) was independently
    developed without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is
    required to be disclosed by law or court order.

7.  WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

20. Vendor Warranties. Vendor represents and warrants that: (a) it has
    the full right and authority to enter into this Agreement and
    perform the Services; (b) the Services shall be performed in
    accordance with industry standards; (c) the Services shall not
    infringe any intellectual property rights of third parties; and (d)
    it shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws
    and regulations.

21. Client Warranties. Client represents and warrants that it has the
    full right and authority to enter into this Agreement and shall
    provide Vendor with accurate information necessary for the
    performance of Services.

8.  GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

22. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in
    accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to
    its conflict of laws principles.

23. Venue and Jurisdiction. The Parties hereby consent to the exclusive
    jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in
    Travis County, Texas for any disputes arising under or relating to
    this Agreement.

24. Dispute Resolution. In the event of any dispute arising under this
    Agreement, the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the dispute
    through good faith negotiations. If the dispute cannot be resolved
    through negotiation within thirty (30) days, either Party may pursue
    available legal remedies.

9.  GENERAL PROVISIONS

25. Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including any exhibits attached
    hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with
    respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior
    negotiations, understandings, and agreements.

26. Amendments. No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision
    of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by
    both Parties.

27. Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be
    invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in
    full force and effect.

28. Assignment. Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the
    prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party
    may assign this Agreement to a successor in connection with a
    merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

29. Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement
    shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when delivered
    personally, sent by confirmed facsimile or email, or three (3) days
    after being sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the
    addresses set forth above or such other address as either Party may
    designate in writing.

30. Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or
    delay in performance under this Agreement due to circumstances
    beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, war,
    terrorism, labor disputes, or governmental actions.

31. Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each
    of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall
    constitute one and the same instrument.
